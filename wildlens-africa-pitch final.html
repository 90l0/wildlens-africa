WildLens Africa is a platform where tourists and documentary crews search for and contact professional wildlife photographers by country, specialty and national park. Not through Instagram. Not through word of mouth. Through a platform built to put your work in front of the people who actually want to hire you.

The Problem

01

Tourists who search "gorilla photographer Uganda" on Google find travel blogs and stock photo sites — not the actual photographers working in Bwindi every week.

02

Your Instagram portfolio gets seen by other photographers. The person booking a two-week Kenya safari has no idea where to find you or how to verify you are worth hiring.

03

Referrals from lodge managers and tour operators work — but they are slow, unpredictable and impossible to scale when you want consistent bookings across multiple parks.

04

Stock platforms take your images, set the price, keep the client relationship and pay you a fraction. You never know who bought your work or why — let alone get a booking out of it.

"The market for professional wildlife photography in Africa is real. The infrastructure to connect photographers with clients is not."

How It Works

Step 01

Build your profile

Upload your portfolio, list the parks and countries you work in and set your specialties. Your profile is your pitch to every tourist who finds the platform.

Step 02

Get found by the right people

Tourists search by location, wildlife type and language. If someone wants a Maasai Mara leopard specialist, your name comes up — not a stock agency.

Step 03

Receive direct inquiries

Tourists contact you directly through the platform. You handle the negotiation. No middlemen taking a cut of a deal they had nothing to do with.

Step 04

Build a verified track record

Reviews from past clients sit on your public profile. A tourist from Germany deciding between two photographers sees your history, not just your photos.

What You Get

A permanent, searchable home for your work and your reputation.

Public portfolio with full attribution

Every image links back to you. No watermark-stripping, no anonymous uploads.

Discovery by park and species

Tourists can filter by national park, wildlife type and country so your work reaches the people planning that specific trip.

Inquiry system built for bookings

Structured contact forms that ask tourists the right questions before they reach you — dates, group size, what they want to photograph.

Verified photographer badge

Credibility signal for tourists who have never worked with a wildlife photographer before and need a reason to trust you.

Social media links that actually display

Your Instagram, YouTube and website sit prominently on your profile — not buried in a footer.

Google-indexed profile page

Your name and specialties are indexed so searches like "gorilla photographer Uganda" can return your actual profile, not a travel blog.

Who this is built for

WildLens Africa is not a stock platform. It is not for casual travel photographers who shoot on weekends. It is for working professionals who operate in the field, know their parks and are ready to receive paying clients.
